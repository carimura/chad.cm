File Base It's still about the people... July 16th, 2026

I recently came across a list of events Iron.io was a part of in some way. It got me thinking about the before times. San Francisco felt so small, and there was this optimism that we were building the future of the whole developer stack. So much of it revolved around events. Docker was announced at a GoSF meetup (hi, Solomon!). Heroku was racing onto the scene at events like Aloha on Rails (hey, James, Blake, et al.!). Developers were discovering Twilio (hi, Carter!), SendGrid (hi, Tim!), PubNub (hi, Todd and Stephen!), Firebase (James!), and so many more great companies at AngelHack and events like it. More than anything, they were meeting great people. I stay in touch with many of them to this day.

I'm older now, with a bunch of kids, so I'm not bouncing from meetup to meetup anymore. But when I do pop into the city, the vibe feels... different. There's still optimism, but also isolation and uncertainty because of how rapidly engineering is changing.

What do you think?

On the positive side, I'm also seeing a revival of things that connect us in person: meetups, coffee shops, Church attendance, run clubs, and more. I see that continuing as AI divides and isolates us.

Anyway, I wanted to archive this list, so here it is!
